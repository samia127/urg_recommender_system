1. Omani Academic Performance (in High School):
    What are the most common/core subjects students take in the final years of high school in Oman? (e.g., Math, Physics, Biology, Chemistry, English, Arabic, etc.)
    The final years of high school in Oman are divided into two main tracks, the Scientific Stream and the Literary Stream. The subjects a student takes depend entirely on the stream they choose.
      
      A. Scientific Stream
      Focus: Prepares students for university majors in science, technology, engineering, and medicine.
       Core Subjects (Mandatory):
      o	Pure Mathematics
      o	Physics
      o	Chemistry
      o	Islamic Studies
      o	Arabic
      o	Social Studies
      o	English
      ·         Elective Subjects: Students must choose two from the general list below.
      o	Important Note: Biology is an essential elective for any student wishing to pursue medicine.
      B. Literary Stream
      Focus: Prepares students for university majors in social sciences and humanities.
      Core Subjects (Mandatory):
      o	Applied Mathematics
      o	Islamic Studies
      o	Arabic
      o	Social Studies
      o	English
      o	Science & Environment
      ·         Elective Subjects: Students must choose three from the general list below.
      C. General Electives (Available to Both Streams)
      ·         Computer
      ·         Arts
      ·         Physical Education
      ·         Music
      ·         Geography
      ·         English Skills
      ·         The World Around Me (History)
      ·         Biology
      o	What is the standard grading scale used? (e.g., percentage out of 100, GPA, etc.)
      Official Letter Grades:
      A: Excellent
      B: Very Good
      C: Good
      D: Pass / Satisfactory
      F: Fail
      Underlying Numerical System:
      The crucial detail is that a student must achieve a total score of at least 50% to pass a course.
      This confirms that the letter grades correspond to a numerical percentage scale (out of 100). While the exact boundaries aren't given, a standard conversion is typically:
      A: 90 - 100%
      B: 80 - 89%
      C: 70 - 79%
      D: 50 - 69%
      F: Below 50%
